Zone annotations

The Impact Hub is being restructured as a question-led, audience-routed experience rather than a single scrolling content page. Every zone moves someone toward a decision or conversion point. Select a zone name to jump to it.

  1. 1 Hero (high-level what we do + urgency)

    Opens with the two root questions as a visual device: Save Sight and Change Lives. Headline lands the scale of the problem ("2 million people in the UK are living with sight loss. We fund the science that stops it and the social change that transforms life with it."). Stats page link and donate widget are visible but not dominant.

    Addresses: Structural goal: improve onward journeys per audience. Content goal: leverage urgent need to donate. Content goal: showcase what's unique about the F4S brand.

    Existing content, restructured

  2. 2 Audience selector "What brings you here?"

    Clearly labelled route-in cards, not hidden in nav: "I want to support the science", "I want to get involved", "I represent a trust or company", "I'm a researcher", "I want to understand the impact of my gift". Each routes to a tailored sub-page or anchor section. This is the routing engine of the page.

    Addresses: Structural goal: improve onward journeys for each audience. Structural goal: ensure each audience sees relevant impact information. Content goal: show where money goes regardless of gift size.

    New component

  3. 3 Save Sight (the science we fund)

    Brief, searchable by condition. Links to funded research pages, A to Z conditions, and the Prof Michaelides interview as social proof. Condition-specific pages are high-value SEO/AEO/GEO targets, so this zone earns its place both editorially and for search.

    Addresses: Structural goal: optimise for SEO, AEO, GEO. Content goal: evidence of scientific research.

    Existing content

  4. 4 Change Lives (the social change we fund)

    Pulls from the three insight reports (Outside, The Unseen, See My Skills) as anchors. Uses lived experience stories to ground the social programmes side of F4S's work. Important for donors who connect with people rather than lab science.

    Addresses: Content goal: evidence of social change. Content goal: stories of impact. Structural goal: improve SEO for thematic / social topics.

    Existing content

  5. 5 Contextual donate widget

    Not generic. The amount-specific impact descriptions already exist on the site, but they're surfaced here in the context of what's been shown above, rather than as a standalone widget. Catches anyone ready to convert without going deeper into a cluster journey.

    Addresses: Content goal: show where money goes regardless of amount. Content goal: leverage urgent need to donate.

    Repurposed component
